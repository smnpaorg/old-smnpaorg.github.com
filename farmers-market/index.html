Farmers Market

The Graham County Farmers Market will open for the season on the Saturday after July 4th through October in the Graham County Health Department Parking Lot, 21 S. Main Street, Robbinsville, NC 28771.

Come sell, buy, and visit!

The Graham County Farmers Market provides space for local farmers, growers, harvester and crafters to sell their locally home grown, homemade and handmade items. Local is defined as Graham County residents, plus growers from adjacent counties within 35 miles of Robbinsville, since we are the closest market for folks in Nantahala, Topton and Deal’s Gap.

Vendors will be given a Graham County Farmers Market memo of understanding, a market information sheet, and are expected to comply with the USDA and North Carolina Department of Agriculture food safety requirements. Growers and handcrafters are welcome to set up each Saturday from July thru October from 8:00 AM-12 Noon.

The Graham County Farmers Market is sponsored by the Graham County Health Department, the Town of Robbinsville, the Graham County Cooperative Extension Service, and Smoky Mountain Native Plants Association (SMNPA). SMNPA is supported by the American Reinvestment and Recovery Act, the Center for Disease Control, Community Foundation of Western North Carolina, The Conservation Fund’s Resourceful Communities Program, North Carolina Rural Economic Development Center, Z. Smith Reynolds Foundation, Office of Economic Opportunity / Catholic Social Services, Blue Ridge National Heritage Area, North Carolina MarketReady, Tobacco Trust Fund, the United States Forest Service, the United States Department of Agriculture, and Burt’s Bees.

For more information please contact: Carol Lawson, SMNPA’s Farmers Market Manager (828) 479-8871or Randy Collins, NC Cooperative Extension (828) 479-7979.
